Problem with Escalade Networks

November 2002

Hello, I was a customer of Escalade Networks. I had a problem with them and am wondering if I am the only one. [Several people have replied to me now with similar horror stories.]

Escalade Networks offered me a dedicated Linux host for a $150 setup fee plus $80/month. It seemd to good to be true, and it was.

The service worked fine from 9/28/2001 until 10/8/2001. On 10/8 I noticed that my web sites were gone. I tried to login and to administer the server but could not access it. I called their technical support which is advertised as "24/7" or always available. I left a message and also sent an e-mail. Over the next few days I left several more messages and sent several more e-mails, and used their online bug report form. On 10/10 I got one letter saying they would contact me but they never did. I sent more e-mails and left messages but they still do not respond. I eventually had to rent a dedicated host from another company and move all my web sites, at some expense. (In contrast, one night I had a problem with the new service and they put 3 engineers to work on it until they fixed it. That is more typical of the industry.)

I sent them a postal letter asking for my money back but they have not responded to that either.

The bottom line is that I paid $230 for a service that they did not provide. And they refuse to respond to my inquiries. Their web site is still up so they seem to be in "business".

February 2002

After getting no reply, I finally asked Visa to do a chargeback. They reviewed my complaint, agreed with me, and refunded the money.

March 2002

For old time sake, I called Escalade Networks and spoke with a fellow named Ryan. I asked him what happened and he said that they had gotten "over-extended last fall but were now back in operation".

April 6th, 2002

I received a letter from the CA Attorney General regarding my complaint. Apparently they are contesting the $230 Visa chargeback. They claim that I "did not know what [I] was doing" and just had trouble logging in because I forgot my password.  They offered no explanation in the letter about why there was no response from tech support for months.

July 12th, 2002

Escalade Networks is no longer in operation. According this e-mail: "Port80 Communications, Inc. has acquired the assets and customer based of [censored] and Escalade Networks, Inc."
