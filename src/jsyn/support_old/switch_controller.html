Here is another possible workaround for the VolumeBug.

Use Other Hard Disk Disk Controller

Since the Promise controller is the source of the problem, an obvious and final solution involves not using it. Many of the new PCs have an on-board IDE controller that is used for CD-ROM drives and other slow IDE devices. In addition, there is a Promise controller in a PCI slot used for the big new hard drive. The fix involves disconnecting your hard drive from the Promise controller and connecting it to the primary IDE controller on the motherboard.

This is the solution recommended by Dell, Gateway, and Promise. It is also the solution that several folks on the bug mail list have ended up doing, including myself. It turns out to be not very hard and really works. There are, however, several reasons why you may not want to do this.

  1. You may not have a second IDE controller in your computer.
  2. The fix involves opening your PC and moving cables around. If you are not comfortable doing this, then find a friendly nerd to do it for you.
  3. The Promise controller is possibly being used for a reason. If the other disk controller is old, it may not support the full size of your hard drive. This could prevent the system from booting.
  4. The other controller may not support your hard drive at its full rate in which case you will have lower performance.
Gateway says my controller (AMD-756) will see the whole drive and run almost as fast as the Ultra100 except for bursts. So I moved my HD off of the Promise controller and haven't noticed any slowdown. Another plus is that I should now be able to run Linux 6.2 which doesn't support ATA100.

First you should determine whether you have a second IDE controller to use. Here's how:

  1. Right click on "My Computer icon and select "Properties"
  2. Click the "Device Manager" tab.
  3. Click on the "+" symbol beside "Hard disk controllers.". If the Promise Ultra controller is here then you may be stuck with it. If you have another product listed here then you are in luck.
  4. Click on the "+" symbol beside "SCSI controllers". You should see the Promise Ultra controller listed here.
If you have a second IDE controller, and wish to proceed at your own risk with this change, then try the following:
  1. Read all instructions through once before starting.
  2. Backup all your important data.
  3. Shutdown PC and open the case.
  4. Discharge any static electricity by touching the bare metal chassis of the PC.
  5. Locate hard drive. Follow ribbon cable to Promise Ultra controller which is a PCI card on my machine. Disconnect ribbon cable from the Promise  controller .
  6. Locate CD-ROM drive. Follow cable to Secondary IDE connector. Primary IDE controller connector is next to it and should be labelled "Primary".
  7. Align red edge of hard drive ribbon cable with red edge of other IDE ribbon cable. Plug hard drive cable gently into the Primary IDE connector.
  8. Remove Promise Ultra controller and place it in a static proof bag or wrap it in aluminum foil.
  9. Turn on PC and immediately start hitting the "F1" function key repeatedly until you see a special BIOS setup screen.
You now want to tell the BIOS to use the onboard IDE controller instead of the Promise controller. This procedure can vary from machine to machine. Your computer probably came with a motherboard manual which may give you some clues. Here are some tips for specific machines:

Asus A7V

Information from David Lomneck , a system builder using ASUS A7V boards:
  1. First make sure you are running Bios Ver. 1004C or higher. Only this version or higher allows you to disable the Ultra100 Controller in the Bios.
  2. After you have entered the bios, under the "boot" tab, you see some options at the  bottom.
  3. You want to disable the ATA and you also want to disable the ATA from being first. These are the last two options on that page.

Gateway Select

  1. Use the down arrow to select "Boot Options" and hit <ENTER>.
  2. Select the "Third Boot Option" which is probably "SCSI Drive" and hit <ENTER>.
  3. Select "1st IDE Drive" and hit <ENTER>.

Dell

  • Bob's Suggests switching from Promise ATA100 to reliable ATA66 controller.
  • Bob's How-To switch from Promise ATA100 to reliable ATA66 on Dell XPS.
Now save your BIOS changes and exit. Your system should continue to boot successfully. Once you have booted, open "My Computer" and make sure all your volumes are there and have the correct size. If so, then you are done. If not, then put your hard drive back on the Promise controller.
